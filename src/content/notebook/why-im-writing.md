There is a quiet cost to ideas that never leave the head. They calcify. They feel right because they have not yet been wrong in front of anyone. The notebook is the antidote — a place where thinking is forced to take a shape someone else could read.

## The shape of a working notebook

A notebook is not an essay. An essay arrives. A notebook is the place where things are still moving. The argument might be wrong, the example might be the wrong example, and the sentence after the next one might cancel the previous one. That is fine. The point is to be working in public, not finished.

I write here for three reasons:

1. **To find out what I think.** Writing is the most reliable way I know to discover the gap between a feeling and a position.
2. **To leave a trail.** The work I do — problem exploration, AI, instrument-building — is faster than memory. The notebook is how I keep up with myself.
3. **To find people.** Some readers become collaborators. Some become friends. None of that happens if the work stays inside.

## What you'll find here

- Long pieces about problems worth working on.
- Shorter notes about specific ideas that are still loose.
- Occasional case studies — instruments I've built, what they did, what they didn't.

The hope is not to be right on every page. The hope is to be useful, and to keep moving.
