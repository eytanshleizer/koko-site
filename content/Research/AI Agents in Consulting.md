## The Wave

AI agents are transforming how consulting and systems integration work gets done. Not in 5 years. Now.

The transition is from:
- **AI as a tool** (Copilot, ChatGPT — helps individuals write faster) 
→ **AI as a worker** (agents that reason, plan, and act across systems autonomously)

This is qualitatively different. The implications for SIs and consulting firms are enormous.

---

## What's Actually Changing

### 1. The unit of work is shifting

Previously: a consultant does 40 hours of discovery, produces a deliverable.
Now: an agent does the research in 2 hours. The consultant reviews, refines, and adds judgment.

The human's job doesn't disappear — it shifts to:
- Framing the problem (judgment)
- Validating the output (expertise)
- Managing the relationship (trust)
- Doing things agents can't yet do (politics, nuance, creativity)

**Implication:** The firms that figure out how to use agents to do the scalable work while humans focus on judgment will 2–3x their consultant output capacity.

---

### 2. Structured knowledge is the unlock

Agents are only as good as their context. An agent with an unstructured PDF and an agent with a structured ontology graph are not the same agent.

- Unstructured → probabilistic text completion → unreliable outputs
- Structured ontology → typed queries + graph reasoning → consistent, traceable outputs

This is why the Customer Intelligence Graph is strategically important: it's the structured context layer that makes agents actually useful in enterprise sales.

---

### 3. The commoditization risk is real

If AI agents make "doing Salesforce implementations" faster and cheaper, what happens to the margins of a firm whose value prop is "we're good at Salesforce implementations"?

They compress. Hard.

Firms that survive the commoditization wave will be those that:
- Own proprietary intelligence (not just process)
- Deliver insight no one else can produce
- Have a learning layer that compounds — getting smarter with every engagement
- Can prove depth, not just delivery

## Who's Winning

### The large players (Global SIs)
- **Accenture:** Acquired NeuraFlash for Agentforce depth, betting heavily on AI agent practices
- **Deloitte:** Building AI agent CoEs, pitching clients on "AI-native transformation"
- **McKinsey:** QuantumBlack + various AI investments — building proprietary data/AI tools
- **IBM:** Watsonx, consulting combined with AI tooling — mixed results
- **Strategy:** Use scale + client relationships to absorb the AI transition

### The acquirers (IT services players)
- **TCS:** Bought Coastal Cloud — Salesforce expertise acquired, not built
- **Cognizant:** Bought Thirdera — ServiceNow Agentforce equivalent
- **Wipro, Infosys, HCL:** Acquiring Salesforce/AI-specialized boutiques globally
- **Strategy:** Buy the expertise while it's still acquirable

### The boutique specialists (Our target buyers)
- **CloudMasonry, Plative, Accelirate** — Racing to prove Agentforce depth before acquired
- **Ascendix, Naitiv** — Betting on vertical depth as a moat
- **Risk:** Can't out-scale the big players, must out-think them

### The disruptors
- **AI-native firms** — small teams using agents to deliver what previously required 50 consultants
- These firms are emerging and represent the long-term existential threat to traditional SIs
- 2–3 people + strong ontology design + good agents = competitive delivery capability

## The Agentforce Moment

Salesforce's Agentforce platform (launched 2024–2025) is the inflection point for Salesforce-focused SIs.

- **What it is:** AI agents deployed within Salesforce — for sales, service, RevOps, etc.
- **What it changes:** Salesforce is no longer just a CRM + automation platform. It's an agent deployment surface.
- **Partner implication:** Partners that can design, build, and deploy Agentforce are valuable. Partners that can't will be commoditized.
- **Timing:** There's a 12–24 month window where Agentforce expertise is rare and premium-priced. After that, it'll be table stakes.

**The Customer Intelligence Graph is Agentforce-native by design** — it maps the customer complexity that Agentforce deployments require, and generates the discovery materials that make Agentforce proposals credible.

## The Commoditization Map

| Activity | Commoditization risk | Timeline |
|----------|---------------------|---------|
| Standard Salesforce config | 🔴 High — agents can do most of it | Already happening |
| Custom Salesforce dev | 🟡 Medium — complex logic still needs humans | 2–3 years |
| Discovery and assessment | 🟡 Medium — agents can research, humans still needed for nuance | Happening now |
| Solution design | 🟢 Lower — judgment, creativity, politics | 3–5 years |
| Change management | 🟢 Low — deeply human | Long term |
| Proprietary IP / tools | 🟢 Moat — compounds against commoditization | Indefinite |

**The insight:** Firms need to shift up the value chain (judgment, IP, relationships) while using agents for the commodity layer (research, documentation, templating).

---

## What Boutiques Should Do Now

1. **Build or buy proprietary intelligence tools** — Something that makes every engagement demonstrably smarter
2. **Develop Agentforce specialization** — The premium pays today, becomes table stakes in 24 months
3. **Create a learning layer** — Institutional knowledge that can't be replicated by a new firm
4. **Position for acquisition on capability terms, not just headcount** — The multiple goes up when you have IP, not just people

**This is exactly the opportunity the Customer Intelligence Graph addresses.**
