Everything unprocessed lands here. Interviews, research, screenshots, bookmarks, half-formed notes. It is a dump. It is supposed to be messy.

The sub-structure is simple:

- `interviews/`
- `research/`
- `screenshots/`
- `bookmarks/`
- `notes/`

Rule: humans write here, never AI. This is your messy thinking space. Voice memos, copy-pasted articles, photos of whiteboards. If the agent wrote it, it does not belong in Raw. The moment AI-generated text leaks into this layer, the signal-to-noise ratio collapses. You cannot curate what you did not author.

The AI's job is to wait. Do not ingest Raw directly. Let the human curate first. The agent reads Frame and Context, not the landfill. This discipline prevents the model from "helpfully" riffing on garbled early notes. It also forces the human to do the upstream work of deciding what matters.

Andrej Karpathy keeps a folder he calls "raw" — unfiltered notes, links, fragments. The genius is not the folder. It is the boundary. Raw is the noise before signal. Respect the boundary, or you get mud at every layer above.
