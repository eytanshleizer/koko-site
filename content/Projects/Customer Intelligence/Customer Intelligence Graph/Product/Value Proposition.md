## The Problem

Every SE and SC at every consulting and SI firm starts from scratch with every new customer.

The process:
1. Google the company. Read their website. 20 minutes.
2. Search LinkedIn. Find some names. 30 minutes.
3. Look at their Salesforce AppExchange reviews. 15 minutes.
4. Read a few Glassdoor reviews. 10 minutes.
5. Skim an earnings call transcript. 20 minutes.
6. Write some notes. Build a rough mental model. 1 hour.
7. Build a prep deck. 2 hours.
8. Do the discovery call. Realize they missed 60% of what mattered.
9. Go back and research more. Another 2 hours.
10. Write the proposal. 5–8 hours.
11. Build the business case. 4–6 hours.
12. Build the exec deck. 3–5 hours.
13. Win or lose. If win: tell delivery what they know. Most of it doesn't transfer.
14. Delivery starts over. Customer re-answers questions they already answered.

**Total: 40+ hours per major customer engagement. Repeated. Every time. By every SE.**

And the output? Generic. Inconsistent. Siloed. Gone when the SE leaves.

---

## The Vision

> Walk into any customer already understanding them better than they understand themselves. Leave with the entire engagement packaged, ready to hand off, already shipping.

The SE who uses this product shows up to discovery already knowing:
- The customer's org structure (who owns what, who the blockers are)
- Their systems landscape (what they run, how it integrates, what's failing)
- Their pain points (by department, by system, in their own language)
- Their strategic context (what leadership told investors last quarter)
- The gaps in the picture (what needs to be confirmed in discovery)

The discovery session becomes a **ratification exercise**, not a research exercise. The customer feels understood. The SE feels prepared. The proposal writes itself from the ratified profile.

## The 3 Value Pillars

### Pillar 1: Speed — 90 minutes vs. 40 hours

| Task | Without | With |
|---|---|---|
| Customer research | 40+ hours | 90 minutes (automated) |
| Proposal draft | 5–8 hours | 30 minutes (generated from profile) |
| Business case | 4–6 hours | 30 minutes (ROI model from profile signals) |
| Executive deck | 3–5 hours | 20 minutes (from profile narrative) |
| Department decks (×3) | 6–9 hours | 45 minutes |
| Discovery guide | 1–2 hours | Auto-generated from profile gaps |
| Handoff package | 2–4 hours | Instant (everything already linked) |
| **Total** | **~60–75 hours** | **~4–5 hours (review + adjust)** |

**The math:** A firm with 10 SEs running 3 major engagements per month = 30 engagements × 60 hours = 1,800 SE hours per month on research and document production. At a fully loaded SE cost of $150/hr: **$270,000/month in addressable productivity waste.**

---

### Pillar 2: Depth — Structured Intelligence vs. Tribal Knowledge

**The problem with manual research:**
- Quality varies by SE skill and effort
- Research lives in personal notes and mental models
- Context is not transferable — it lives with the person
- No structure — can't be queried, compared, or compounded

**What structured intelligence means:**
- Every customer is profiled to the same schema
- Research is auditable — every claim has a source and confidence score
- Profiles are queryable — "show me all customers with ServiceNow-Salesforce integration issues"
- Context transfers — new SE picks up a customer profile and immediately has full context
- Intelligence compounds — patterns across profiles become firm-level knowledge

**Depth analogy:** Manual research is a sketch. The Profile Engine is a detailed architectural drawing. Both describe the same building. Only one is useful when something goes wrong.

---

### Pillar 3: Compounding — Every Engagement Makes the Next One Better

This is the pillar that justifies the price.

**Without this product:**
- Engagement 500 is as hard as Engagement 1
- Every SE reinvents the discovery process
- Institutional knowledge lives in people's heads and churns out the door with them

**With this product:**
- Engagement 50 is sharper than Engagement 1
- Engagement 500 is dramatically sharper than Engagement 50
- The [[Global Learning Layer]] accumulates patterns that the system automatically applies to new profiles
- The firm has a proprietary intelligence asset that gets more valuable over time

**This is what makes it worth $5M, not $50K:** A $50K tool gives you speed. A $5M asset gives you compounding competitive intelligence that improves every year and sits on your balance sheet.

**Total annual value: ~$5.1M/year**

**Product cost: $5M (one-time IP transfer)**

**Payback period: ~12 months**

**5-year NPV (10% discount): ~$18M**

## Competitive Landscape

### What exists today
| Competitor | What they do | Why they're not this |
|---|---|---|
| **Salesforce Einstein** | AI features inside Salesforce | Only works inside SF data — no external customer research |
| **ZoomInfo / Bombora** | Contact data + intent signals | Firmographic, not structural — no systems, workflows, or pain |
| **Gong / Chorus** | Call recording + deal intelligence | Captures intelligence after meetings, doesn't generate it beforehand |
| **Klue / Crayon** | Competitive intelligence | About your competitors, not your customers |
| **ChatGPT / Claude (DIY)** | LLM-based research | Unstructured, un-schemaed, doesn't persist, no learning layer |
| **Manual SE research** | Human effort, tab-hopping | Doesn't scale, doesn't persist, doesn't compound |

### The honest answer: nothing does this
- There is no product that builds a structured, interactive, visual customer model from public signals
- There is no product that links that model to all downstream artifacts
- There is no product that compounds that intelligence into a firm-level asset
- The competitive answer is: this is genuinely novel

### What that means for the sale:
- No "we already have this" objection (unlike most software sales)
- The objection is "we don't believe this works" — counter with demo
- Or "we don't need this" — counter with the ROI model and the compounding argument

## Strategic Value

### Beyond ROI: the exit multiple argument

See [[Sales Strategy]] and [[Global Learning Layer]] for full detail.

**The pitch:**
- TCS paid ~$700M for Coastal Cloud. Accenture acquired NeuraFlash.
- Every independent Salesforce firm is in a **12–24 month acquisition window**.
- What gets you top-of-range multiples? Proof of thinking depth + proprietary IP.
- The Customer Intelligence Graph puts a **proprietary intelligence asset on your balance sheet**.
- A firm with 3 years of compounded vertical intelligence is worth more than a firm with the same headcount and no IP.

### The balance sheet argument:
- SI firms traditionally have no hard assets — value is in headcount, which walks out the door
- A learning layer is actual IP with demonstrable, auditable, compounding value
- M&A buyers value AI/intelligence assets at **3–5x revenue multiples above comparable firms**

### The competitive moat argument:
- Once the learning layer has 50 engagements in a vertical, competitors can't catch up by buying the same product
- They would need 3 years of their own engagements to reach the same intelligence depth
- The moat is time-based — which means acting now compounds more than acting later

## Objection Handling

### "We already do discovery"
**Response:** Yes — and you're good at it. But this isn't about replacing your discovery process. It's about showing up to discovery already 80% done, so the session becomes confirmation and refinement instead of starting from scratch. Your SEs can have smarter conversations because they're not spending 40 hours building context. They're using those 40 hours for client relationships.

---

### "Our SEs know their customers"
**Response:** The best ones do. But the knowledge lives in their heads, not in a system. When they leave, that knowledge leaves. When you onboard a new SE, they start over. And the 5 SEs who are good but not great? They're operating at half the depth of your best person. This product raises the floor for everyone — and it makes your best people's knowledge the firm's knowledge, not just their own.

---

### "This seems expensive"
**Response:** Let's run the math. [Use ROI model above]. The productivity savings alone pay it back in 12 months. The win rate improvement adds $2M+ per year in revenue. The exit multiple argument adds $5–15M in terminal value when you sell. The question isn't whether $5M is a lot — it's whether **$18M in 5-year NPV** makes $5M a good investment. And that's the conservative model.

---

### "We don't trust AI for something this important"
**Response:** Neither do we — which is why the system doesn't replace your SEs, it gives them better starting material. Every profile is reviewed and ratified by a human before it touches a customer. The mirror moment is where your SE — not the system — walks the customer through the profile and confirms it. The AI does the 40-hour research job. The SE does the 2-hour sense-check. You get the best of both.

---

### "What if the research is wrong?"
**Response:** It will be partially wrong — that's expected and designed for. Every field has a confidence score. Low-confidence fields are flagged before you show them to a customer. The discovery session is specifically designed to correct what the research got wrong. After correction, the profile is ratified and accurate. Over time, the [[Global Learning Layer]] makes the initial research more accurate — so the corrections get smaller every year.
