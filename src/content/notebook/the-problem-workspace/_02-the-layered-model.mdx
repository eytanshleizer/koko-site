Information has gravity. It also has velocity. Mix fast noise with slow truth and you get mediocre decisions.

The solution is five layers. Each has a purpose, a cadence, and an editor.

> **Raw → Frame → Context → Work → Review**

**Raw** is unprocessed input. Human-only. **Frame** is the structure you impose on that input. Human-led, AI-assisted. **Context** is the curated background the agent needs to reason. Joint authorship. **Work** is the artifact being produced. AI-led, human-edited. **Review** is the feedback loop that closes the cycle. Human judges, AI adjusts.

Each layer is edited by a different combination of human and machine. No layer is optional. Skip Frame, and your context is bloated. Skip Review, and your work drifts. Every layer is a quality gate. Treat one as a checkbox, and the whole stack rots.

> **Key insight:** The layers are not folders. They are contracts about who writes, who reads, and who decides.
