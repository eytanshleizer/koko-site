---

### Discovery: What CIG Asks

The first CIG session with Accelirate's engagement lead and solution architect surfaces the following:

**Process Discovery Questions:**
- What are the 10 most common repeatable processes in patient ops today?
- Which of those involve a human making a judgment call (vs. just following a rule)?
- Where do processes break down — handoff gaps, missing data, exception queues?
- What does a "good" outcome look like vs. a stuck case?

**Automation Inventory Questions:**
- Which bots exist, what triggers them, and what do they actually touch?
- Are bots reading from EMR directly or from a Salesforce layer?
- Which bots fail most often, and what happens when they fail?
- Where do humans step in to fix bot failures?

**Governance & Compliance Questions:**
- What data is an AI agent allowed to read? Write? Act on?
- Who is accountable when an agent makes a wrong decision?
- What audit trail requirements does the compliance team need?
- Are there specific HIPAA-relevant data flows that must stay human-touched?

**Transformation Vision Questions:**
- What does "fully automated" look like in 18 months?
- What processes are off-limits politically, even if technically automatable?
- What's the tolerance for an agent being wrong 2% of the time vs. 0.1%?

After 90 minutes of structured discovery, CIG renders the client's process landscape as a graph — and Accelirate's consultant holds it up to the client.

The graph shows:

3. **Four [[Decision Point]] nodes** where a human is making a binary choice that could be modeled as a classifier — these are the AI Agent Candidate zones
4. **Two [[Compliance Requirement]] constraints** that fence off certain data paths entirely
5. **One massive [[Data Silo]]** — the EMR system — that every downstream process depends on but nobody can query in real time

**The mirror moment:** The client sees their own operation for the first time as a structured system rather than a collection of habits. The pain isn't in any one process — it's in the topology. The graph makes that visible.

Accelirate's consultant now has a precise, evidence-based brief for the Agentforce architecture. Instead of a generic "AI agents can help" pitch, they're saying: *"Here are the four specific places where an AI agent replaces a human decision, here are the two compliance fences we design around, and here's the integration sequence."*

**That's the sell.** CIG doesn't just organize information — it produces the narrative that closes the next phase.
