The frame is the most expensive layer. A bad frame wastes **100%** of everything that follows.

> A frame is a selected way of seeing a problem — the boundaries, the rules, and the win condition.

This is where raw material becomes a problem statement. Not one problem statement. Many. The workspace holds multiple frames simultaneously because ambiguity is real. It doesn't collapse just because you want closure.

The structure is deliberate:

```
frames/
├── frame-01-customer-churn/
│   ├── problem.md
│   ├── constraints.md
│   ├── stakeholders.md
│   └── questions.md
├── active-frame/
└── archived-frames/
```

Every frame folder contains the same template. `problem.md` states what you're solving. `constraints.md` lists what you can't change. `stakeholders.md` maps who cares. `questions.md` records what you still don't know.

The human writes the first frame. The AI critiques it. It asks the questions you forgot. It finds the constraints you assumed away.

Most teams skip this. They jump to solutions. The workspace doesn't let you.
