<p class="about-intro">
I'm <strong>Eytan Shleizer</strong>, a solutions engineer based in Israel. I work at the intersection of enterprise technology and real human problems—helping companies figure out what to build, how to build it, and whether AI actually makes it better.
</p>

<div class="about-section">

## Who I Am

I've spent my career in the space between "we have a problem" and "here's a working solution." That means discovery calls, architecture diagrams, technical demos, proofs of concept, and the inevitable conversations about what's actually feasible versus what the slide deck promised.

I'm drawn to complexity. Not for its own sake—I just find that the interesting problems are rarely simple ones.

</div>

<div class="about-section">

## What I Do

Most of my work falls into three buckets:

**Solutions Engineering** — Taking vague business requirements and turning them into something technical that actually works. Discovery, scoping, architecture, demos, and the long process of getting to "yes" with enterprise buyers.

**AI Deployment** — Helping organizations move past the "we should use AI" phase into production systems. LLMs, RAG, agents, and all the unglamorous integration work that makes them useful.

**Building** — When the tools don't exist, I build them. Internal tools, automation, AI-native workflows. I prototype fast and iterate until it works.

</div>

<div class="about-section">

## What I'm Building

Right now I'm deep into AI agents and personal AI infrastructure. I'm especially interested in systems that help humans stay in control while automating the tedious stuff—not replacing human judgment, but augmenting it.

I write about this on the [blog](/posts). Topics include AI-native consulting, the reality of enterprise AI adoption, sales engineering, and whatever else I'm thinking about.

</div>
